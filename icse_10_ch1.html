%1. GST Basics & Reading a GSTIN▾
GST
Implemented 1st July 2017 (Articles 246A, 269A, 279A). Every sale is taxed at one of five slabs: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%.
GST Amount
\( \text{GST} = \dfrac{\text{Rate}}{100}\times\text{Taxable Value} \)
Price including GST
\( \text{Total Price} = \text{Taxable Value} + \text{GST} \)
⇄2. Intra-state vs Inter-state▾
Intra-state (same state)
\( \text{CGST} = \text{SGST} = \dfrac{1}{2}\times\text{Total GST} \)
Inter-state (different states)
\( \text{IGST} = \text{Total GST} \)
Board trap
GST is always charged on the price AFTER any discount — never on the marked price itself.
⛓3. Trading Chain & Input Tax Credit (ITC)▾
Net GST Payable
\( \text{Net GST} = \text{Output GST} - \text{ITC} \)
Output GST = GST on the seller's own selling price. ITC = GST already paid on their purchase (passed on by the previous seller).
WHY THIS EQUALS "TAX ON PROFIT"
1. GST at any stage \( = \text{Output Tax} - \text{ITC} \)

2. GST at any stage \( = \text{Tax on S.P.} - \text{Tax on C.P.} = \text{Tax on (S.P.} - \text{C.P.)} = \text{Tax on Profit} \)

3. Total GST received by Government \( = \) Tax paid by the consumer
Reading this
Line 2 is just algebra: since tax is a fixed rate × value, subtracting two tax amounts is the same as taxing the difference of the two values. That's the entire justification for the "rate × profit" shortcut used throughout this worksheet.
Why the "rate × profit" shortcut works
Output GST − ITC always collapses to rate × (SP − CP), i.e. rate × profit. Both the ITC method and the profit-margin shortcut are shown side by side in every chain question below.

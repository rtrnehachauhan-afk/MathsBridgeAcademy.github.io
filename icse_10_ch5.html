Theory & Formulae
📄1. Standard Form of a Quadratic Equation▾
Standard Form
\( ax^2+bx+c=0,\ \ a\neq 0 \)
a, b, c are real numbers; a is the coefficient of \(x^2\), b of x, and c is the constant term.
⚠️ MBA Note: If the highest power of x after full expansion is not 2, the equation is NOT quadratic (could be linear or higher degree).
🧮2. Solving Methods▾
Factorisation (splitting the middle term)
\( ax^2+bx+c=0 \)
Split b into two parts whose product is \(a\times c\) and whose sum is b, then factor by grouping.
Quadratic Formula
\( x=\dfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a} \)
Works for every quadratic equation, even when factorisation is not obvious.
Which method to use
Try factorisation first — it is quicker when the roots are rational. If the numbers don't split cleanly, or the question says "using the quadratic formula" / "correct to 2 decimal places", go straight to the formula.
🔎3. Nature of Roots (Discriminant)▾
Discriminant
\( D=b^2-4ac \)
Value of DNature of Roots
\( D>0 \), a perfect squareReal, unequal and rational
\( D>0 \), not a perfect squareReal, unequal and irrational
\( D=0 \)Real and equal
\( D<0 \)No real roots (imaginary)
⚠️ MBA Note: "Equal roots" always means D = 0 — set up that equation whenever a question asks for an unknown that makes the roots equal.
🔗4. Framing an Equation from Given Roots▾
From two roots \(\alpha,\beta\)
\( (x-\alpha)(x-\beta)=0 \)
Expand and simplify to standard form. If the roots are fractions, clear denominators at the end.
Verifying a root
\( \text{Substitute } x=\text{given value into LHS; if LHS}=\text{RHS, it is a root} \)
Finding an unknown coefficient from a given root
\( \text{Substitute the given root into the equation and solve for the unknown} \)
📝5. Word Problems — Setting Up the Equation▾
THE PATTERN
Assign a variable (usually x) to the unknown quantity asked for, express every other quantity in the problem in terms of x, translate the given condition into an equation, solve it, and reject any root that does not fit the physical situation (e.g. negative length, negative age, negative number of people).
Organise before you equate
Whenever a problem gives several related quantities (ratios, speeds, ages, costs), list them in a simplification table first — it prevents mixing up which expression belongs to which unknown before the equation is framed.
⚠️ MBA Note: Always check both roots against the real-world condition (e.g. "natural number", "positive speed") before writing the final answer — reject the root that doesn't fit.

📄1. What is an Arithmetic Progression?▾
ARITHMETIC PROGRESSION (A.P.)
A sequence of numbers in which the difference between any two consecutive terms is always the same. This fixed difference is called the common difference, written d. If the first term is a, the AP looks like a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, …
SymbolMeaning
aFirst term of the A.P.
dCommon difference = (any term) − (the term just before it)
nNumber of terms / the position of a term
tₙ (or aₙ)The nth term (general term)
lLast term of the A.P.
TypeExampleSign of d
Increasing2, 5, 8, 11, …d > 0
Decreasing20, 17, 14, 11, …d < 0
Constant5, 5, 5, 5, …d = 0
⚠️ MBA Note: To check if a sequence is an A.P., subtract EVERY consecutive pair — one matching pair isn't enough, all of them must give the same d
🧮2. The nth Term (General Term)▾
nth term formula
\( t_n = a + (n-1)d \)
Gives the value of the term sitting at position n, without listing every term before it.
WORKING BACKWARDS — "WHICH TERM IS X?"
Set tₙ equal to the given value and solve the same formula for n instead of for the term. n must always come out as a positive whole number — if it doesn't, that value is not a term of the A.P.
FINDING a AND d FROM TWO GIVEN TERMS
Write tₙ = a+(n−1)d for each given term — this gives two linear equations in a and d. Subtract one equation from the other so a cancels, leaving d directly; then substitute back to find a.
Two A.P.s with equal nth terms
Write the nth-term expression for each A.P. in terms of n, set them equal, and solve for n — then substitute back into either expression to get the common term value.
Σ3. Sum of n Terms (Sₙ)▾
Sum formula — when a, d, n are known
\( S_n = \dfrac{n}{2}\big[2a+(n-1)d\big] \)
Sum formula — when the last term l is known
\( S_n = \dfrac{n}{2}(a+l) \)
Faster whenever the last term is already stated in the question — no need to find d first.
Recovering a term from two sums
\( t_n = S_n - S_{n-1} \)
Useful when the question gives Sₙ as a formula/expression in n rather than giving a and d directly.
⚠️ MBA Note: "Sum of first 15 terms" and "sum of NEXT 15 terms" are different quantities — the second is S₃₀ − S₁₅, not S₁₅ again
🎯4. The Smart Substitution Trick▾
WHY IT WORKS
When a question gives the SUM of a small, specific number of terms in A.P. (and asks you to find the actual numbers), don't use a, a+d, a+2d, … Instead centre the terms symmetrically around the middle so d cancels out of the sum completely — this finds a instantly.
No. of termsTake the terms asSum simplifies to
3 termsa−d, a, a+d3a
4 termsa−3d, a−d, a+d, a+3d4a
5 termsa−2d, a−d, a, a+d, a+2d5a
After finding a
Use the SECOND piece of given information (product of terms, sum of squares, a ratio, etc.) to form an equation in d and solve for it. Then convert the symmetric terms (a−d, a, a+d, …) back into the actual numbers of the sequence.
⚠️ MBA Note: The "d" in the symmetric trick is HALF the true common difference of the original sequence when 4 terms are spaced a−3d,a−d,a+d,a+3d (each real term is 2d apart) — always convert back to the real sequence at the end
